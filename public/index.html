TULLAHAN RIVER MONITORING

Get the latest news and updates about your local river.

In recent months, floods have worsened with a series of typhoons that affected the Philippines. Whereas water level monitoring used to be done manually. Local authorities near the river must record levels of water on a fixed, or often monthly basis. The residents must wait and watch news to get updates regarding the Tullahan River. Our goal is to make TuRiMo as an easy to access river monitoring website. Users can quick check the status of the river. Aside from time-saving benefits, the TuRiMo Web App can provide even greater insights of Tullahan River.

LEVEL 1

When the water level reaches 12 inches that is equivalent to 8 feet that indicates that the water level hits LEVEL 1 -Low Risk; together with a sound alarm that has a 1-second duration and 10 seconds delay with an audio frequency of 440hz.

group

LEVEL 2

When the water level reaches 8 inches that is equivalent to 5 feet that indicates that the water level hits LEVEL 2 - Moderate Risk; together with a sound alarm that has a 5-second duration and 10 seconds delay with a frequency of 620hz.

settings

LEVEL 3

When the water level reaches 4 inches that is equivalent to 3 feet that indicates that the water level hits hits LEVEL 3 - High Risk; together with a sound alarm that has a quick whooping sound with a frequency of 440hz.

The reading here shows how far the proximity sensor to the water level.
